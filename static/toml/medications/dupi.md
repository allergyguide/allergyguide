## 2. Clinical Pharmacology

- **Mechanism of Action:** `[Brief 1-2 sentences]`
  - IL-4R antagonist (blocks IL-4 and IL-13)
- **Pregnancy & Lactation:** `[Provide safety summary - add links if you have them]`
  - Minimal data. IgG does pass through the placenta. No information on breastfeeding.
- **Half-Life:** `[Optional - e.g. "~6 hours", "15 days", etc.]`
  - After steady state, median washout time "_9-13 weeks in adults and adolescents and are approximately 1.5 times and 2.5 times longer in pediatric subjects 6 to 11 years of age and pediatric subjects less than 6 years of age, respectively_"
  - Do note that:
    - No clinical studies have been conducted to evaluate the effect of hepatic impairment on the pharmacokinetics of dupilumab.
    - No clinical studies have been conducted to evaluate the effect of renal impairment on the pharmacokinetics of dupilumab. Population PK analysis did not identify mild or moderate renal impairment as having a clinically meaningful influence on the systemic exposure of dupilumab.

## 3. Safety & Monitoring

- **Contraindications:** `[Optional - List absolute/relative contraindications]`
  - None relevant aside from hypersensitivity
- **Severe Interactions:** `[Optional - e.g., CYP3A4 inhibitors]`

No severe interactions, but did note reduced metoprolol metabolism. Not studied with live vaccines. Non-live vaccines were fine.

```
In a clinical trial with 12-13 evaluable subjects with atopic dermatitis, the effects of dupilumab injection on the pharmacokinetics of caffeine (metabolized by CYP1A2), warfarin (metabolized by CYP2C9), omeprazole (metabolized by CYP2C19), metoprolol (metabolized by CYP2D6), and midazolam (metabolized by CYP3A4) were evaluated. The AUC of metoprolol increased by 29% after dupilumab injection administration (a SC loading dose of 600 mg followed by 300 mg SC weekly for 6 weeks). The AUC of other CYP substrates investigated were comparable before and after dupilumab injection administration.

DUPIXENT has not been studied with live vaccines.
```

- adult patients with atopic dermatitis is an initial dose of 600 mg (two 300 mg injections), followed by 300 mg every 2 weeks (Q2W)

  - 15 to less than 30 kg 600 mg (two 300 mg injections) => 300 mg every 4 weeks

  - 5 to less than 15 kg: 200 mg (one 200 mg injection) then 200 mg every 4 weeks
  - 15 to less than 30 kg: 300 mg (one 300 mg injection) then 300 mg every 4 weeks

### asthma

The recommended dose of DUPIXENT for adults and adolescents (12 years of age and older) is: • An initial dose of 400 mg (two 200 mg injections) followed by 200 mg given every 2 weeks (Q2W) for patients with severe asthma with a type 2/eosinophilic phenotype. The dose may be increased to 300 mg every-other-week based on clinical judgement. • An initial dose of 600 mg (two 300 mg injections) followed by 300 mg given 2 weeks (Q2W)for patients with oral corticosteroids-dependent asthma or with co-morbid moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis or adults with co-morbid severe chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis for which DUPIXENT is indicated.

The recommended dose of DUPIXENT for children (6 to 11 years of age) is :
15 to less than 30 kg - 300 mg every four weeks (Q4W)
30 to less than 60 kg - 200 mg every 2 weeks (Q2W) or 300 mg every 4 weeks (Q4W)
60 kg or more - 200 mg every 2 weeks (Q2W)
no initial loading dose for kids in the age range 6 to 11

Does not significantly increase the risk of serious bacterial or opportunistic infections

The DUPIXENT pre-filled pen is only for use in adults and pediatric patients aged 2 years and older. The prefilled pen autoinjector does hurt more than the syringe.

The DUPIXENT pre-filled syringe with needle shield can be used in adults and pediatric patients aged 6 months and older.
